Safety Tips for Your Adventure

General Safety Tips

Guided Tours: Always join guided tours led by experienced and knowledgeable local guides, especially when exploring remote areas. Guides are familiar with the terrain and can ensure your safety while providing important information about the environment.
Always follow your guide’s instructions.

First Aid: Carry a basic first aid kit that includes essentials like antiseptic, bandages, pain relievers, and any personal medications you may need. Guides will also have more advanced first aid supplies.

Weather Awareness: Royal Belum experiences tropical weather, with occasional rainfall and storms. Keep an eye on the weather, especially if you're planning outdoor activities like kayaking or hiking. If a storm is predicted, activities may be postponed or canceled for safety.

Hiking & Trekking Tips

Royal Belum offers many hiking and trekking opportunities through dense forests, which can be challenging for some:

Wear Appropriate Footwear: Ensure you wear proper hiking boots or shoes with good grip. The trails can be muddy, slippery, and uneven, so sturdy footwear is crucial.

Stay on Trails: The terrain in Royal Belum can be steep, muddy, and uneven, especially during or after rain. Make sure you're physically prepared for the hike, and if you're not comfortable with the difficulty level, choose a shorter or easier route.

Wether Precautions: Always check the weather before setting out, as tropical rainstorms can occur unexpectedly. If you hear thunder or see dark clouds approaching, seek shelter immediately.

Know Wildlife Hazards: Be cautious of potential wildlife encounters, including snakes, wild boars, or large mammals. Stay with the group, avoid disturbing wildlife, and listen to your guide’s advice on handling such encounters.

Kayaking Safety

Kayaking is a popular activity in Royal Belum, especially on Temengor Lake and surrounding rivers, but it requires certain precautions:
Wear A Life Jacket: Always wear a life jacket while kayaking. Even if you’re an experienced kayaker, the waters can be unpredictable, and the life jacket will provide essential buoyancy in case of capsizing.

Avoid Fast Currents: Stick to calmer waters or areas where the current is manageable. Strong currents in rivers or sudden changes in the weather can pose significant risks.

Don't Kayak Alone: Never kayak alone in unfamiliar areas. Always travel with others, especially when exploring remote or difficult-to-access parts of the park.
Safety Briefing: Pay attention to any pre-trip safety briefings provided by your guide. Learn how to paddle correctly, how to handle capsizing, and what to do in case of an emergency
