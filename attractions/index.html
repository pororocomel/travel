Cultural Insight
Orang Asli Communities

The Orang Asli are Malaysia's indigenous people, and their presence in Royal Belum adds a layer of cultural richness to the park. The Semai and Jahai tribes are the primary indigenous groups in this region. Visitors can learn about their traditional lifestyles, including their:

  • Agricultural Practices: The Orang Asli practice subsistence farming, growing crops like rice, tubers, fruits, and vegetables. Visitors can observe their agricultural methods, often using sustainable practices passed down through generations.
  • Hunting and Gathering: Traditionally, the Orang Asli also hunted for food in the forests and gathered forest products like fruits, herbs, and medicinal plants. While hunting has become less frequent due to government regulations, they still rely on the forest for materials and medicines.
Artisanal Skills
Traditional Crafts and Skills

The Orang Asli excel in crafting bamboo and wood products, weaving, and creating intricate decorative items.

  • Bamboo and Wood Crafting: The Orang Asli are skilled in making practical and decorative items from bamboo and wood. These include tools, baskets, and sometimes intricate carvings. Visitors may have the opportunity to witness these crafts being made or even participate in crafting workshops.
  • Weaving: The weaving of baskets and mats is another traditional skill practiced by the Orang Asli. Visitors may be able to purchase handmade items or try their hand at weaving with the guidance of local artisans
Nature & Culture
Nature and Culture Integration

In Royal Belum, nature and culture are deeply intertwined. The Orang Asli view the forest as sacred and integral to their way of life. Forests are not only places for food and shelter but also serve as spaces for spiritual practices and rituals. Many aspects of their culture are based on a profound respect for nature and its resources, making Royal Belum an ideal destination for those seeking to understand how indigenous cultures live in harmony with the environment.

Natural Wonder
Caves

Royal Belum State Park is known for its natural beauty and rich biodiversity, but it is also home to a variety of caves that add another fascinating dimension to the area. These caves, formed over millions of years, are not only geological wonders but also hold cultural and historical significance. They are often located within the dense rainforest or near the lake, making them somewhat remote and challenging to access.
