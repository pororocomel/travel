History

Discover Royal Belum's Legacy

A journey through time, from the traditions of the Orang Asli to its establishment as a state park.

Early History and the Orang Asli

Royal Belum has been home to indigenous groups like the Temiar and Jahai for centuries. These communities live in harmony with the forest, using sustainable practices that preserve the ecosystem and maintain their cultural heritage.

Their traditional knowledge and deep connection to the land highlight the area's rich history. The Orang Asli communities, such as the Jahai and Temiar, live in the surrounding areas. Visitors can interact with them and learn about their traditional lifestyles and cultures.

Colonial Era

During British rule, the forests were heavily exploited for timber, leading to deforestation. The establishment of forest reserves like Temenggor aimed to protect timber resources and manage the natural landscape.

Despite these efforts, large-scale deforestation shaped the region's modern boundaries.

Creation of Royal Belum

In 1993, the Perak State Government designated Royal Belum as a forest reserve. By 2007, it was declared a state park, emphasizing conservation efforts and biodiversity protection.

Today, Royal Belum stands as one of Malaysia's most important ecological and cultural treasures.
