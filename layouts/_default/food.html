Delight in the Flavors of Royal Belum

Savor the best local cuisines that reflect the heart and culture of Perak.

Temengor Grilled Fish (Ikan Bakar)

The Temengor Lake area is known for fresh fish, and you can enjoy delicious grilled fish or ‘ikan bakar’. This dish typically features a fish caught from the lake, marinated with spices, and grilled to perfection. It’s often served with a side of sambal or ulam a traditional Malaysian salad, rice, and a refreshing sour-sweet sauce.

Where to try it: You may find restaurants or food stalls in Banding Island offering freshly grilled fish from the lake, especially if you go on a fishing or boating tour. Grilled fish is often a popular dish in Gerik as well.

Nasi Lemak Royal Belum

Nasi Lemak is one of Malaysia's most iconic dishes, and you will likely find it served in various eateries around Gerik and other nearby towns. It consists of fragrant coconut rice served with spicy sambal which is chili paste, crispy anchovies, and a boiled egg. It can be accompanied by fried chicken, beef rendang, or other proteins to suit your taste.

Where to try it: You can find nasi lemak at local roadside stalls or small cafés in near the entrances to Royal Belum. Some of the guesthouses or eco-lodges in the area might also serve it as part of their breakfast offerings.

Cendol Perak

Cendol is a beloved Malaysian dessert, perfect for cooling off after a day of exploring the tropical jungle. It consists of green rice flour jelly, coconut milk, and palm sugar syrup, served with crushed ice. The combination of creamy coconut milk and sweet palm sugar makes for a refreshing treat, especially in Malaysia’s warm climate.

Where to try it: Gerik and nearby towns have local stalls serving delicious cendol. It’s commonly found in food markets, street food stalls, and even at small roadside cafés.

Local Fruits

While you’re in the Royal Belum area, you may come across a variety of fresh, tropical fruits. The region is known for durian, mangosteen, rambutan, and other seasonal fruits, which may be available at local markets or from roadside vendors.

Where to try it: In Gerik, you can visit fruit markets to taste fresh, local produce. During fruiting seasons, you’ll likely find these fruits being sold along the roadsides leading into Royal Belum.
